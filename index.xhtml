Jordanian Arabic: Spoken Grammar Variations

About the Project

The project focuses on patterns used in the Jordanian dialect of Arabic to analyze how speakers use varying grammer structures. It examines authentic snippets of text from Jordanians, taken from the Facebook page called Humans of Amman, as its corpus. The project mainly focuses on three grammar concepts:

  1. The use of dialectical prefixes in verbs
  2. Gender agreement among noun/adjective phrases
  3. Prepositions that link to the following words

Details

The project was desinged for the Spring 2016 semester of HIST 1030 Computational Methods in Humanities at the University of Pittsburgh.

Amber Montgomery is a junior at the University of Pittsburgh studying History, International Studies, and Arabic.
